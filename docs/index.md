## Introduction

The GDIL Research Program investigates a foundational question for artificial intelligence and computational science:

> **Given a computational goal and a universe of available computable knowledge, what knowledge should be applied?**

Modern computing provides unprecedented computational capability, yet little infrastructure exists for discovering, aligning, and composing computational knowledge in response to goals. The program explores this problem through new conceptual models, experimental methods, computational corpora, and open software.

---

## Research Themes

The program currently focuses on four closely related areas.

### Computable Knowledge

Formal representations of computational capability as reusable scientific objects.

### Goal Alignment

Methods for identifying computational knowledge relevant to a stated computational objective.

### Goal-Directed Intelligence Layers

A conceptual computational layer supporting discovery, alignment, and eventual orchestration of computable knowledge.

### Experimental Infrastructure

Reference specifications, corpora, and software supporting reproducible investigation of goal-directed intelligence.

## Research Direction

The long-term objective of the GDIL Research Program is to establish scientific foundations for computational environments capable of discovering, aligning, and ultimately orchestrating computable knowledge at scales substantially beyond direct human comprehension.
