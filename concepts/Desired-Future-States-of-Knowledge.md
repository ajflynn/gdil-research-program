# Desired Future States of Knowledge (DFSKs)

## Definition

A Desired Future State of Knowledge (DFSK) is a target knowledge state specified by a computational goal.

A DFSK describes what is intended to become known when a computational goal is successfully achieved.

Examples include:

| Computational Goal | Desired Future State of Knowledge |
|--------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Estimate risk | Risk estimate is known |
| Predict outcome | Predicted outcome is known |
| Classify entity | Classification is known |
| Determine quantity | Quantity is known |
| Select alternative | Preferred alternative is known |

A computational goal specifies a DFSK.

A DFSK is realized when the computational goal is achieved.

## Why It Matters

The DFSK concept provides a goal-centered way of thinking about knowledge application.

Rather than focusing on computational processes themselves, DFSKs focus attention on the knowledge state that computation seeks to produce.

This perspective allows diverse computational activities to be understood through a common conceptual framework.

## Relationship to Other Concepts

DFSKs are directly connected to:

- Computational Goals
- Goal-Directed Knowledge Application
- Goal–Knowledge Alignment
- Constructed Realizations

Computational goals specify DFSKs.

Goal-directed knowledge application seeks to realize them.

## Role in the GDIL Research Program

DFSKs serve as the conceptual anchor for goal representations used throughout the GDIL Research Program.

They provide a domain-independent framework for expressing computational objectives and play a central role in GARI experiments involving goal–knowledge alignment.
