# Goal-Directed Knowledge Application

## Definition

Goal-Directed Knowledge Application is the process of aligning and applying computable knowledge in pursuit of explicit computational goals.

Unlike approaches focused on generating new knowledge from observations, Goal-Directed Knowledge Application assumes the existence of computable knowledge and focuses on its systematic application.

The objective is to realize desired future states of knowledge specified by computational goals.

## Why It Matters

Many knowledge systems focus on storing, discovering, executing, or governing computable knowledge.

Goal-Directed Knowledge Application introduces a different perspective.

The central question becomes:

> How can existing computable knowledge be systematically aligned and applied to achieve explicit computational goals?

This shift places goals at the center of knowledge application activities.

## Relationship to Other Concepts

Goal-Directed Knowledge Application depends upon:

- Computational Goals
- Desired Future States of Knowledge
- Goal–Knowledge Alignment
- Computable Knowledge

GDILs provide infrastructure intended to support Goal-Directed Knowledge Application.

## Role in the GDIL Research Program

Goal-Directed Knowledge Application is the principal activity that GDILs are intended to support.

Many research activities within the program ultimately relate to determining whether metadata and other mechanisms can reliably align computable knowledge with goals.

The concept therefore serves as a bridge between conceptual foundations and experimental investigations.
